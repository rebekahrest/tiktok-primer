TikTok Ethics Primer

How Does TikTok Make Money?

Advertisements


TikTok makes money through advertising revenue. In 2022, TikTok is predicted to make almost $10 billion in ad revenue, more than double what it generated in 2021.1 TikTok has a large user base of more than 1 billion users worldwide, and evidence shows users are highly engaged.2 TikTok’s users “spend an average of 96 minutes a day on the app — nearly five times what they spend on Snapchat, triple their time on Twitter and almost twice as much as their time on Facebook and Instagram.”3 The app heavily relies on user engagement to make money. When users spend more time on the app, they view more sponsored ads, so time on the platform directly translates to higher profits. Thus developers have an incentive to keep users on the platform for as long as possible.

Ads’ Influence on Users

Due to TikTok’s highly engaged viewer base, the platform has had large success in attracting advertising business. Companies spent nearly $100 million on advertising on TikTok since the beginning of this year.4 For users, it is important to keep in mind that the purpose of the app is to make money. While the app’s curation of content is fun and highly engaging, ultimately advertisements and sponsored content are pushed to users to influence them in some way, whether to have a certain opinion about a company or to make them purchase something. TikTok’s ads are unique compared to other social media platforms because they appear the same as any other video on the platform, so they are not immediately recognizable as ads. On one hand, users benefit because the app’s personalization algorithm makes advertisements more meaningful to users, and they can discover products that they really like that they would not have known about otherwise. However, users are more susceptible to being unconsciously influenced by the app because it selectively personalizes advertisements to match users’ interests and disguises them to look like regular content. Ads become more powerful because the personalization algorithm makes them more relevant to users and thus more influential. Judging by companies’ large investments in advertising on the app, they also recognize its power as a highly effective advertising tool. Accordingly, potential users should be aware of corporate influence on the content they view.
