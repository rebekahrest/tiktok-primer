TikTok Ethics Primer

What is TikTok?

Overview


TikTok is a free social media app for making and sharing short videos. Users navigate through the app by scrolling up and down through an endless feed of full-screen videos. According to TikTok’s website, "TikTok is the leading destination for short-form mobile video," and their mission is "to inspire creativity and bring joy."1 TikTok’s videos tend to be short, but they can run up to a maximum of 10 minutes. There is a wide variety of content available, ranging from jokes, dances, fashion advice, cooking tips, travel inspiration, product recommendations, restaurant reviews, medical advice, and more. The app makes use of hashtags, which function as organizational categories for users to search for content. They make it easy to find videos for various challenges, viral dances, or variations of repeated jokes. Users can easily find similar videos about a topic they are interested in or search for content for inspiration for making their own videos.

Content Creation

Tiktok provides several tools for video creators. They can use interactive filters similar to Snapchat to add visual enhancements to videos, such as presets to change the color of videos or effects to change face shape, add makeup, or overlay animal faces. Users can also add background music to videos. TikTok provides a searchable library of music to easily find and add music. Tiktok makes it easy to create videos because of the extensive prompts it provides. Along with filters and sound effects, it provides video clips from TV shows, YouTube videos, or other TikToks to spur ideas for content creators.2

How is TikTok different from other social media platforms?

TikTok has some similar features to other social media apps such as being able to follow users and providing direct messaging functionality. However, TikTok is different from other social media apps because it is centered around a content feed called “For You,” which shows content that it determines a user would be interested in based on what they have watched in the past. While other apps such as Instagram show content from users’ friends, TikTok focuses on showing content based on users’ viewing habits, making it more machine-driven. Users are encouraged to jump between different content creators and trends rather than focusing on a certain social network of friends and acquaintances.
