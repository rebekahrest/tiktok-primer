TikTok Ethics Primer

Misinformation


TikTok’s algorithm promotes the visibility of popular content and adds to its virality through trending hashtags. This gives equal opportunity for any user, no matter their credentials, to create content on any topic, and spread information quickly with enormous reach. The app offers enormous power to ordinary citizens to have a voice, but also provides more visibility to uncredible sources. When young, uneducated, or malicious users are given such power, it opens up the possibility for misinformation to spread quickly. Reports show that “well crafted lies” get more engagement than factual content, and TikTok’s algorithm contributes to the spread of misinformation because of its basis in user engagement.1 Compared to other social media, the app has additional capabilities for amplification of misinformation since the app is built around the “For You” page of video recommendations instead of a user’s social network. For example, the hashtag #StopTheSteal trended widely during the 2020 election, spreading baseless conspiracy theories about voter fraud, while posts circulated earlier this year claiming that predictions of a surge in Covid-19 cases in the fall were an attempt to discourage in-person voting.2 Other falsehoods that have spread include myths about the deadliness of Covid-19 vaccines, claims the mass shooting in Uvalde was staged, and false accounts of the war in Ukraine.3

People who use social media as their main news source are at risk of consuming and believing false information, as it can be hard to separate the truth from fabrication. To counteract the spread of misinformation, users must be aware that misinformation exists and that it is likely they will encounter it at some point while using the app. It is important for users to corroborate information from social media with other reputable sources, such as news publications. Users should also evaluate the credibility of the accounts when determining if information is trustworthy. To help individuals identify authentic accounts representing a public figure, TikTok identifies verified accounts with a blue checkmark icon. Users can also counteract the spread of misinformation by reporting misleading posts. Overall, users must approach any video promoting sensational news with a degree of skepticism and work to verify the information before trusting others blindly.
